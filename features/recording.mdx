Meetily provides powerful audio recording capabilities that capture both your microphone and system audio simultaneously. All recordings are processed locally on your device, ensuring complete privacy.

## Starting a Recording

There are multiple ways to start recording in Meetily:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click the Record Button">
    Click the red microphone button in the center of the home screen to begin recording.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Or use the Sidebar">
    Click the record icon in the sidebar to quickly start a new recording from anywhere in the app.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Grant Permissions">
    On first use, you'll need to grant microphone and screen recording permissions (for system audio capture).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  **Permission Requirements**
  - **Microphone**: Required for capturing your voice and meeting participants
  - **Screen Recording** (macOS): Required for capturing system audio (like meeting software)
</Info>

## Recording Controls

Once recording starts, you'll see the recording control bar at the bottom center of the screen:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Pause/Resume" icon="pause">
    Pause recording temporarily and resume when ready. Paused segments are indicated with an orange color.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Stop Recording" icon="square">
    Stop the recording and begin processing transcripts. The app will save your audio and generate transcripts automatically.
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Audio Levels" icon="signal">
    Visual bars show real-time audio input levels, confirming that audio is being captured.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Audio Sources

Meetily captures audio from two sources simultaneously:

### Microphone Audio
Captures your voice and any sounds picked up by your selected microphone device.

### System Audio
Captures audio from your computer's applications, perfect for recording:
- Video conferencing calls (Zoom, Teams, Google Meet)
- Webinars and presentations
- Screen recordings with audio
- Any audio playing through your computer

<Warning>
  **System Audio Setup Required**
  
  To capture system audio, you need a virtual audio device:
  - **macOS**: Install [BlackHole](https://existential.audio/blackhole/) (free)
  - **Windows**: Built-in WASAPI loopback support
  - **Linux**: Configure PulseAudio or ALSA loopback
</Warning>

## Professional Audio Mixing

Meetily uses advanced audio processing to ensure high-quality recordings:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="RMS-Based Ducking">
    Automatically reduces system audio volume when you speak, ensuring your voice is always clear and prominent in the recording.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Clipping Prevention">
    Intelligent volume management prevents audio distortion even when both microphone and system audio are loud.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="48kHz Sample Rate">
    Professional-quality audio recording with consistent sample rates across all audio sources.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

| "Speech detected" | Voice activity detected |

## Voice Activity Detection (VAD)

Meetily uses Voice Activity Detection to optimize transcription:

- Only processes audio segments containing speech
- Reduces transcription workload by ~70%
- Improves transcription speed and accuracy
- Automatically filters out silence and background noise

<Tip>
  **First Recording Tip**
  
  The first recording after launching Meetily may take a few extra seconds to start as the transcription model loads into memory. Subsequent recordings will start instantly.
</Tip>

## Recording Best Practices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Test Your Audio">
    Before important meetings, do a quick test recording to verify both microphone and system audio are working correctly.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Check Audio Levels">
    Watch the audio level bars when you speak - they should show activity without maxing out.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Minimize Background Noise">
    Record in a quiet environment for best transcription accuracy.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Name Your Meetings">
    Give your recording a descriptive name so it's easy to find later in your meeting library.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recording Storage

All recordings are stored locally on your device:

- **Development**: `frontend/recordings/` directory
- **Production (macOS)**: `~/Library/Application Support/Meetily/recordings/`
- **Production (Windows)**: `%APPDATA%\Meetily\recordings\`
- **Format**: High-quality WAV files

<Info>
  Recordings are automatically cleaned up after 7 days to save disk space. Make sure to export any important recordings before then.
</Info>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="No audio being captured">
    - Verify microphone permissions are granted
    - Check that the correct input device is selected in Settings
    - Ensure no other app is using the microphone exclusively
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="System audio not recording">
    - Install BlackHole or appropriate virtual audio device
    - Configure system audio routing to include virtual device
    - Grant screen recording permissions (macOS)
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Recording fails to start">
    - Check that a transcription model is loaded in Settings
    - Verify you have sufficient disk space
    - Try restarting the application
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
