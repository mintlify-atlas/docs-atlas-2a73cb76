# Speaker Diarization

Speaker diarization is the process of automatically identifying "who spoke when" in an audio recording. This advanced feature helps you understand conversation flow and attribute statements to specific participants.

## Availability

<Warning>
**PRO Feature**: Speaker diarization is currently available only in **Meetily PRO**. The Community Edition does not include this capability.
</Warning>

### Community Edition

The open-source Community Edition focuses on privacy-first transcription and AI summaries without speaker identification. All transcripts are generated as a continuous stream without speaker labels.

### Meetily PRO

Meetily PRO includes advanced speaker identification capabilities:

- **Automatic speaker detection**: Distinguishes between different voices
- **Speaker labeling**: Tags each segment with speaker identifiers (Speaker 1, Speaker 2, etc.)
- **Timeline visualization**: See who spoke at what time
- **Custom speaker names**: Assign real names to detected speakers

## How It Works

Speaker diarization uses acoustic features to cluster speech segments by speaker:

1. **Voice Activity Detection (VAD)**: Identifies speech vs. silence
2. **Speaker Embedding**: Extracts unique voice characteristics
3. **Clustering**: Groups segments by speaker similarity
4. **Labeling**: Assigns speaker identifiers to each segment

## Use Cases

### Team Meetings

Track individual contributions and follow up on specific action items by person:

- "What did Sarah say about the deadline?"
- "Review all comments from the product manager"
- "Find Alex's feedback on the design"

### Interviews & Research

Distinguish between interviewer and subject responses for qualitative analysis.

### Multi-Party Discussions

Understand conversation dynamics in group settings:

- Board meetings with multiple executives
- Panel discussions
- Workshop sessions with facilitators and participants

## Limitations

### Current Constraints

Even in PRO, speaker diarization works best under certain conditions:

- **Clear audio**: Minimal background noise improves accuracy
- **Distinct voices**: Similar voices may be harder to distinguish
- **Non-overlapping speech**: Simultaneous speakers are challenging
- **Minimum speech per speaker**: Each speaker needs sufficient audio samples

### Privacy Considerations

Speaker identification creates additional privacy implications:

- **Biometric data**: Voice prints are considered biometric identifiers in many jurisdictions
- **Consent requirements**: Some regions require explicit consent for voice identification
- **Data retention**: Consider policies for storing speaker embeddings

## Alternatives in Community Edition

### Manual Speaker Attribution

While Community Edition doesn't automate speaker identification, you can:

1. **Review raw transcripts**: Listen to recordings alongside transcripts
2. **Manually annotate**: Add speaker names during editing
3. **Use meeting context**: Leverage meeting metadata (attendees, agenda)
4. **AI summary insights**: LLM summaries may infer speakers from context

### Example Workflow

```markdown
1. Record meeting with all participants
2. Generate transcript (speaker-agnostic)
3. Generate AI summary
4. Review summary for attributed quotes
5. Cross-reference with your notes to identify speakers
```
