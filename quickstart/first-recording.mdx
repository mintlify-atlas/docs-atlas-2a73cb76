This guide walks you through recording your first meeting with Meetily, from launch to viewing your AI-generated summary.

## Prerequisites

Before you begin, make sure you have:
- Installed Meetily on your device ([macOS](/installation/macos) | [Windows](/installation/windows) | [Linux](/installation/linux))
- A working microphone connected to your computer
- (Optional) A virtual audio device for system audio capture

<Info>
**First Launch Tip** — On macOS, you'll be prompted to grant microphone and screen recording permissions. These are required for audio capture.
</Info>

## Step-by-Step Guide

<Steps>
  <Step title="Launch Meetily">
    Open Meetily from your Applications folder (macOS), Start Menu (Windows), or application launcher (Linux).

    The main interface displays:
    - Recording status in the center
    - Sidebar with meeting history (left)
    - Audio level indicators
    - Settings icon (gear) in the top-right corner
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure Transcription Model">
    On first launch, you'll need to select a transcription model:

    1. Click the **Model Settings** button or the settings gear icon
    2. Choose your transcription provider:
       - **⚡ Parakeet** (Recommended) — Real-time, accurate, optimized for speed
       - **🏠 Local Whisper** — High accuracy, more processing time
    
    <Tip>
    **Parakeet** is recommended for real-time transcription. It's 4x faster than Whisper while maintaining excellent accuracy.
    </Tip>

    ### Downloading Your First Model

    If using **Parakeet**:
    - The `parakeet-tdt-0.6b` model will be available for download
    - Click **Download** and wait for completion (100-200 MB)
    - The model downloads to your local machine — no cloud required

    If using **Local Whisper**:
    - Choose from models: `tiny`, `base`, `small`, `medium`, or `large-v3`
    - **Recommended**: Start with `base` (140 MB) for balanced speed and accuracy
    - Larger models provide better accuracy but require more processing power

    <Note>
    Model downloads happen only once. They're stored locally in:
    - **macOS**: `~/Library/Application Support/Meetily/models/`
    - **Windows**: `%APPDATA%\Meetily\models\`
    - **Linux**: `~/.local/share/Meetily/models/`
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure Audio Devices (Optional)">
    To ensure the best recording quality:

    1. Click the **Audio Settings** button (speaker icon)
    2. Select your **Microphone** from the dropdown
       - Choose "Default Microphone" or a specific device
       - Look for the microphone you want to use
    3. Select your **System Audio** device (optional)
       - On macOS: Requires BlackHole or similar virtual audio device
       - On Windows: WASAPI loopback captures system audio automatically

    <Tip>
    **Test Your Microphone** — Speak into your microphone and watch the audio level indicator. Green bars indicate good input levels.
    </Tip>

    [Learn more about audio setup →](/quickstart/audio-setup)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Start Recording">
    1. (Optional) Enter a meeting name in the text field
       - If left blank, Meetily generates a name like "Meeting - Jan 15, 2026"
    2. Click the large **Start Recording** button
    3. The button changes to **Stop Recording** with a red indicator

    **What happens when you start:**
    - Audio begins capturing from your selected devices
    - Voice Activity Detection (VAD) identifies speech segments
    - Transcription starts in real-time (visible in the right panel)
    - Audio is mixed professionally with ducking and clipping prevention
  </Step>

  <Step title="Monitor Live Transcription">
    As you speak, transcription appears in real-time on the right side:

    - **Colored dots** indicate transcription confidence:
      - 🟢 Green: High confidence (80-100%)
      - 🟡 Yellow: Medium confidence (60-80%)
      - 🔴 Red: Low confidence (below 60%)
    - Timestamps show when each segment was spoken
    - Transcripts update continuously as speech is detected

    <Info>
    **Real-time Processing** — Parakeet transcribes with minimal delay. Whisper processes in 30-second chunks, so transcription appears every 30 seconds.
    </Info>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Stop Recording">
    When your meeting ends:

    1. Click the **Stop Recording** button
    2. Meetily processes the final transcripts (takes 5-10 seconds)
    3. The recording is saved automatically with:
       - Complete audio file (WAV format)
       - Full transcript with timestamps
       - Meeting metadata (date, duration, device info)

    <Warning>
    **Don't Close Meetily** immediately after stopping. Wait for "Recording saved successfully" confirmation to ensure all data is preserved.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

  <Step title="View Your Recording">
    After stopping, your recording appears in the left sidebar:

    1. Click on the meeting name in the sidebar
    2. The meeting details page opens showing:
       - **Full transcript** with timestamps
       - **Audio playback** controls
       - **Export options** (copy, download)
       - **AI Summary** button (if AI provider configured)

    <Tip>
    **Keyboard Navigation** — Use the sidebar to quickly switch between meetings. Your most recent recordings appear at the top.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate AI Summary (Optional)">
    If you've set up an AI provider, you can generate meeting summaries:

    1. Open your completed meeting from the sidebar
    2. Click **Generate Summary** button
    3. Wait 10-30 seconds for summary generation
    4. The summary appears with sections:
       - **Key Points**
       - **Action Items**
       - **Decisions Made**
       - **Next Steps**

    [Learn how to set up AI summaries →](/quickstart/ai-setup)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Troubleshooting First Recording Issues

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="No transcription appears during recording">
    **Possible causes:**
    - Microphone not selected or not working
    - Transcription model not downloaded completely
    - Voice Activity Detection (VAD) threshold too high

    **Solutions:**
    1. Check **Audio Settings** to confirm microphone is selected
    2. Verify model download completed in Model Settings
    3. Speak clearly into the microphone — VAD requires audible speech
    4. Check system microphone permissions (macOS: System Preferences > Security & Privacy > Microphone)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Recording stops unexpectedly">
    **Common reasons:**
    - Audio device disconnected
    - System ran out of disk space
    - GPU/CPU overload on low-powered devices

    **Solutions:**
    1. Ensure audio devices remain connected during recording
    2. Check available disk space (recordings are ~10 MB per minute)
    3. Use a lighter transcription model (Whisper `tiny` or `base`)
    4. Close other resource-intensive applications
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Audio levels are too low or too high">
    **Fix low audio:**
    - Increase system microphone input level
    - Move closer to the microphone
    - Use an external microphone with better sensitivity

    **Fix clipping (too loud):**
    - Reduce system microphone input level
    - Move farther from the microphone
    - Meetily's professional audio mixing includes clipping prevention, but extreme levels can still distort
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Model download fails or is very slow">
    **For Whisper models:**
    1. Check your internet connection
    2. Models download from Hugging Face servers — try again if connection was interrupted
    3. Manual download: Place model files in `models/` directory

    **For Parakeet models:**
    1. Similar to Whisper, ensure stable internet connection
    2. The model is downloaded from ONNX repositories
    3. Download progress shows in Model Settings
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Understanding Recording Status

Meetily shows different states during recording:

| Status | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **Ready** | App is ready to start recording |
| **Recording** | Actively capturing audio and transcribing |
| **Stopping** | Processing final transcripts after stop |
| **Processing** | Finalizing transcripts and saving files |
| **Saving** | Writing audio and transcript data to disk |
| **Saved** | Recording successfully saved |

<Note>
**Performance Tip** — GPU acceleration (Metal on macOS, CUDA/Vulkan on Windows/Linux) speeds up transcription by 5-10x. Meetily automatically detects and uses available GPU capabilities.
</Note>
